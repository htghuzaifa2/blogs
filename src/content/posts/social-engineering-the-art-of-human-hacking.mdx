You can spend millions on firewalls, encryption, and biometric scanners. But if a hacker calls your receptionist pretending to be the CEO and asks for a password "urgently," your millions are wasted.

**Social Engineering** is the art of manipulating people into performing actions or divulging confidential information. It targets the "human OS," which hasn't been patched in thousands of years.

## The Psychological Triggers
Attackers exploit standard human behaviors:
1.  **Urgency**: "Your account will be deleted in 5 minutes if you don't verify!" Panic makes us skip logic.
2.  **Authority**: "This is the VP of Finance. Transfer the funds now." We are programmed to obey authority.
3.  **Reciprocity**: "Here is a free gift. Now, can you help me with a small survey?" We feel obliged to return favors.

## Types of Attacks

### 1. Phishing
The most common attack. Sending emails that look legitimate (e.g., from "Netfl1x" instead of "Netflix") to steal credentials.
- **Defense**: Hover over links before clicking. Check the sender's email address carefully.

### 2. Spear Phishing
Targeted phishing. The attacker researches you on LinkedIn, knows your boss's name, and crafts a hyper-personalized email.
- **Defense**: Verify unusual requests through a second channel (e.g., call your boss).

### 3. Vishing (Voice Phishing)
The attacker calls you. They might use AI voice changers to sound like someone you know.
- **Defense**: Never give passwords or OTPs over the phone. Banks will *never* ask for them.

### 4. Baiting
Leaving a malware-infected USB drive labeled "Layoffs 2025" in the company parking lot. Curiosity kills the cat (and the network).

## Building a Human Firewall
- **MFA (Multi-Factor Authentication)**: Even if they trick you into giving your password, they can't get in without your phone.
- **Skepticism**: "Trust but verify."
- **Slow Down**: Urgency is the red flag. Take a breath and think.

## Conclusion
Technology cannot solve social engineering. The only defense is education and a culture of security awareness.
