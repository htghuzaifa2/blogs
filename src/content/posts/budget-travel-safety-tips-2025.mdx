## 2. The Art of "Split Funding"

Never keep all your cash and cards in one place. This is a classic for a reason.
-   **The Three-Way Split:** 
    1.  **The Day Wallet:** A small amount of cash and one "Low Limit" credit card for daily expenses.
    2.  **The Deep Stash:** A backup card and larger bills hidden in a money belt or sewn into your backpack.
    3.  **The Digital Reserve:** Keep a digital copy of your cards (Apple Pay/Google Pay) and ensure your primary bank account has "International Withdrawals" disabled until the moment you actually need to use an ATM.

## 3. Dealing with Scams in 2025

Scams are becoming more sophisticated. 
-   **The AI Voice Scam:** Be wary if you get a call or a message that sounds like a friend or traveling companion in trouble. AI can now "Clone" voices from a 3-second social media clip. Always have a "Code Word" with your family and travel partners to verify identity in an emergency.
-   **The "Closed" Tourist Attraction:** If a taxi driver or "Helpful" local tells you the museum or temple is "Closed for a holiday today," don't believe them. Go check for yourself. They are usually trying to redirect you to a shop where they get a commission.

## 4. Accommodation Safety: The 2025 Vetting

Hostels are great, but you need to be smart.
-   **Review Mining:** Don't just look at the 5-star reviews on Booking.com. Search for keywords like "Security," "Lockers," or "Theft" in the 2-star and 3-star reviews. 
-   **Bring Your Own Lock:** Never rely on the locks provided by a hostel. Bring a high-quality, heavy-duty combination lock. Avoid key-based locks, as keys are easy to lose or steal.
